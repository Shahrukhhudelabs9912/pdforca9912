Lost your physical Aadhaar card? Need a digital copy for a bank KYC form, a passport application, or a job portal? You don't need to visit an Aadhaar centre — you can download an official e-Aadhaar PDF from the UIDAI website in under 3 minutes.

This guide covers both methods: the UIDAI portal and DigiLocker. Both give you a legally valid Aadhaar PDF that's accepted everywhere the physical card is.

## e-Aadhaar vs. Physical Aadhaar: What's the Difference?

The e-Aadhaar PDF downloaded from UIDAI is **legally equivalent** to the physical Aadhaar card. It carries the same 12-digit number, your photo, address, and a digitally signed QR code that institutions can verify.

| Feature | Physical Aadhaar | e-Aadhaar PDF |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| Legal validity | ✅ Valid | ✅ Equally valid |
| Accepted for KYC | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| Can be verified by QR | ❌ Older cards lack it | ✅ Always has a signed QR |
| Easy to share digitally | ❌ Need to scan | ✅ Ready to upload |
| Password-protected | N/A | ✅ Yes (first 4 letters of name + birth year) |
| Updated address reflected | ❌ Need reprint | ✅ Always latest version |

**Pro tip:** The e-Aadhaar always reflects your latest details (address, phone number). If you updated your Aadhaar recently, the downloaded PDF has the updated info — even if your physical card still shows the old details.

## Method 1: Download from UIDAI (myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in)

This is the official method. Works on any browser — phone or laptop.

### Step 1: Visit the UIDAI Portal

Go to **myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in** (the official UIDAI self-service portal).

### Step 2: Enter Your Aadhaar Number

- Enter your **12-digit Aadhaar number** or **16-digit Virtual ID (VID)**
- Complete the CAPTCHA verification
- Click **Send OTP**

### Step 3: Verify With OTP

- An OTP is sent to the **mobile number linked to your Aadhaar**
- Enter the OTP within the time limit
- If your mobile number isn't linked, you'll need to visit an Aadhaar centre to link it first (this is a one-time requirement)

### Step 4: Download the PDF

After OTP verification, you'll see the option to download your e-Aadhaar. The PDF downloads to your device.

### Step 5: Open the Password-Protected PDF

The downloaded PDF is password-protected. The password is:

**First 4 letters of your name (IN CAPS) + birth year**

Example: If your name is **Rahul Kumar** and you were born in **1995**, the password is: `RAHU1995`

- Only the first 4 letters of your name as printed on Aadhaar
- Always in UPPERCASE
- Birth year in 4 digits (YYYY format)

Enter this password to open and view the PDF.

## Method 2: Download from DigiLocker

DigiLocker is the government's official digital document wallet. If your Aadhaar is linked, downloading is even simpler.

### Step 1: Open DigiLocker

Go to **digilocker.gov.in** or open the DigiLocker app (available on iPhone and Android).

### Step 2: Log In

- Sign in with your DigiLocker account (phone number + OTP)
- If you don't have an account, create one — it takes 2 minutes and requires your Aadhaar number

### Step 3: Find Your Aadhaar

- Go to **Issued Documents** or search for "Aadhaar"
- Your Aadhaar should appear automatically if your Aadhaar number is linked to your DigiLocker account
- If it doesn't appear, go to **Partner → UIDAI → Aadhaar** and pull it

### Step 4: Download

Click the download button. DigiLocker provides the Aadhaar as a PDF — this version may or may not be password-protected depending on the issuing authority.

**DigiLocker advantage:** Documents from DigiLocker carry a digital signature that many government portals can verify automatically. Some portals even accept a DigiLocker URI (link) instead of a file upload.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "Mobile number not registered"

You can only download e-Aadhaar if your mobile number is linked to your Aadhaar. If it isn't:

1. Visit your nearest Aadhaar Enrollment Centre
2. Request a mobile number update (carry your current phone and one ID proof)
3. The update takes 2–10 working days
4. Once linked, you can download e-Aadhaar via OTP

### "OTP not received"

- Check that you're entering the correct number linked to Aadhaar (not your current number if you changed it)
- Wait 30–60 seconds — OTPs sometimes have network delays
- Try "Resend OTP" — you get a few attempts
- If using a dual-SIM phone, make sure the linked SIM is active and has network

### "Password not working"

The password format catches many people. Remember:

- First **4 letters** of your name only (not your full name)
- Must be in **UPPERCASE**
- Followed by your **4-digit birth year** (not age, not DD/MM)
- No spaces between the letters and year

If your name on Aadhaar is "MOHAMMAD IQBAL" and birth year is 1990, the password is `MOHA1990`.

If your name has fewer than 4 letters (e.g., "RAM"), use all available letters: `RAM1990`.

### "PDF is corrupted or won't open"

- Try downloading again — the first download may have been interrupted
- Try a different browser (Chrome often works best)
- Clear your browser cache and retry
- If the problem persists, try the DigiLocker method instead

## Privacy Reminder

Your Aadhaar contains sensitive personal information. After downloading and using the PDF:

- **Delete copies** from your Downloads folder after uploading to the portal
- **Don't share** your Aadhaar PDF over unsecured email or public WhatsApp groups
- **Don't post** images of your Aadhaar on social media
- If you must share digitally, use the **masked Aadhaar** option on UIDAI (shows only last 4 digits of your number)
- **Use VID** (Virtual ID) instead of your actual Aadhaar number when possible — VID provides identity verification without exposing your real number

## FAQ

**How many times can I download e-Aadhaar?**
Unlimited. You can download it as many times as you need.

**Is there a fee for downloading?**
No. Downloading from UIDAI and DigiLocker is completely free.

**Can I download someone else's Aadhaar?**
Only if the OTP goes to a phone you have access to (i.e., the number linked to that Aadhaar). You can help a family member download theirs using their phone for OTP.

**Is the e-Aadhaar accepted for address proof?**
Yes. e-Aadhaar is valid as both identity and address proof wherever physical Aadhaar is accepted.

**My Aadhaar has my old address — can I still use it?**
For identity proof, yes. For address proof, you need to update your address on Aadhaar first (visit an enrolment centre or use the online address update on UIDAI).

**Can I download Aadhaar without a linked mobile number?**
No. OTP verification requires a linked mobile number. Visit an Aadhaar centre to link your current number first.
