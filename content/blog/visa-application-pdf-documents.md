You finally booked the appointment at the consulate. The checklist they sent back has fourteen items on it — bank statements, employment letter, hotel bookings, flight itinerary, passport copy, photographs, and more. Each document came from a different place: your bank's portal, your HR department, a travel agent's email, a government website. Now you need to submit them as a single organized PDF, or as clearly labeled individual PDFs, depending on what the consulate requires.

Getting this wrong costs you the appointment slot. Consulates reject applications for something as simple as a document being in the wrong format, exceeding the file size limit, or having pages in the wrong order. The good news is that preparing your visa documents as clean, organized PDFs takes about twenty minutes once you know the process.

This guide walks through exactly how to do it — from gathering your documents to merging them into a submission-ready file — using free tools that work in any browser, with no software to install and no account to create.

## What You'll Need

- All your visa documents (scanned or already in PDF/JPG format)
- A web browser on your phone or laptop
- No signup, no app, no payment

## Understanding What Consulates Actually Want

Before you start combining files, it helps to know what format most consulates expect. Requirements vary, but these patterns are consistent across Schengen, US, UK, Canada, and Australia visa applications:

| Requirement | What it means for you |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Single PDF per category | Bank statements as one PDF, employment letter as another |
| All documents in one PDF | Everything merged in the order listed on the checklist |
| File size limit (usually 2–5 MB) | You may need to compress large scans |
| Readable text, not blurry scans | Scan at 150–200 DPI, not maximum resolution |
| Correct page order | Checklist order, not the order you found the files |

The most common mistake is submitting a ZIP file or a folder of images. Consulates want PDFs. If your documents are JPGs or PNGs — scanned pages, photos of documents — you need to convert them to PDF first.

## Step-by-Step: Prepare Your Visa Documents as PDF

### Step 1: Collect and Sort All Documents

Go through the consulate's checklist line by line. For each item, locate the file on your device or download it fresh. Rename each file clearly — `bank-statement-3months.pdf`, `employment-letter.pdf`, `hotel-booking.pdf` — so you can find them quickly.

If any document is a physical paper (a bank statement you printed, a letter from your employer), scan it. Most smartphones have a built-in document scanner in the camera or notes app. Scan at 150–200 DPI — high enough to be readable, low enough to keep the file size manageable.

### Step 2: Convert Any Images to PDF

If some of your documents are JPG or PNG files — scanned pages, photos of your passport, screenshots of bookings — convert them to PDF before merging.

Open the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf), upload your image files, and download the resulting PDFs. You can convert multiple images at once. Each image becomes a page in the PDF, so if a document has multiple pages, upload all the images together in the right order.

### Step 3: Merge All Documents into One PDF

Once every document is in PDF format, use the [Merge PDF tool](/merge-pdf) to combine them.

1. Open the [Merge PDF tool](/merge-pdf)
2. Upload all your PDF files
3. Drag to reorder them to match the consulate's checklist order
4. Click Merge and download the combined PDF

The result is a single PDF with all your documents in the exact order the checklist specifies. This is what most consulates want when they say "submit your application as a PDF."

### Step 4: Check the File Size

Most consulate portals cap uploads at 2–5 MB. A merged document with several scanned pages can easily exceed this. Check the file size before uploading.

If it's too large, run it through the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf). This reduces the file size while keeping the document readable. Aim for a result under the portal's stated limit, with a little room to spare.

### Step 5: Review Before Submitting

Open the merged PDF and page through it once before uploading. Confirm:

- Every document from the checklist is present
- Pages are in the correct order
- All text is readable and not blurry
- No pages are upside down or cut off

If a page is rotated wrong, use the [Rotate PDF tool](/rotate-pdf) to fix it, then re-merge. A few minutes of review here prevents a resubmission request later.

## For Indian Applicants: Common Documents and Tips

Indian visa applicants typically need to include several documents that have specific format requirements. Here's how to handle the most common ones.

**Aadhaar card.** Download the e-Aadhaar PDF from the UIDAI website. It's already a PDF, so it merges directly. If you're using a physical copy, scan both sides onto one page.

**PAN card.** Scan both sides. If the portal asks for a single-page document, combine front and back into one PDF page before merging into the main package.

**ITR (Income Tax Return).** Download the ITR acknowledgment from the Income Tax e-filing portal as a PDF. Include the most recent two or three years if the checklist asks for them — merge them in chronological order.

**Bank statements.** Most Indian banks let you download statements as PDF from net banking. Three to six months is the standard requirement. If you have statements from multiple accounts, merge them into one PDF with the most recent month first.

**Employment letter and salary slips.** These often come as Word documents or email attachments. Convert them to PDF with the [Word to PDF tool](/word-to-pdf) before merging.

**File size on Indian portals.** Many Indian government and embassy portals have strict 5 MB limits, and some go as low as 2 MB. Scanned documents at high resolution can be large. Scan at 150 DPI rather than 300 DPI, and compress the final merged PDF if needed.

## Tips for the Best Result

- **Follow the checklist order exactly** — consulates notice when documents are out of sequence
- **Scan at 150–200 DPI** — high enough to read, low enough to keep file size manageable
- **Convert images to PDF before merging** — use the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf) for photos and scans
- **Compress if you're near the size limit** — the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf) reduces size without visible quality loss
- **Name files clearly before uploading** — `bank-statement-june-2026.pdf` is better than `scan0047.pdf`
- **Keep the original PDF** — until you've confirmed the submission was accepted
- **Download fresh copies of digital documents** — bank statements and ITR acknowledgments downloaded the day before submission are more current than copies saved months ago

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Submitting a ZIP file or folder** — most portals only accept PDF
- **Wrong page order** — always match the checklist sequence
- **Blurry scans** — scan at a reasonable resolution; too low is unreadable, too high bloats the file
- **Expired documents** — check validity dates on bank statements, employment letters, and insurance
- **Missing pages** — a three-month bank statement means all three months, not just the most recent
- **Ignoring size limits** — a rejected upload wastes your appointment slot
