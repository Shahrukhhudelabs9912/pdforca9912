Applying for a driving license online in India used to mean a day lost at the RTO. Now most of it happens on the Sarathi portal from your phone — the learner's license test, the application, the fee payment, the slot booking. But there is one step that still trips up almost everyone: the document upload.

The portal wants your address proof, your age proof, and your passport photo and signature, each as a file of the right type and under a strict size limit. Upload a 6 MB photo of your Aadhaar card and the page throws an error you cannot quite decode. Upload the wrong format and it rejects it silently. Suddenly a five-minute task becomes an afternoon of frustration.

This guide fixes that. It walks you through turning your documents and photos into clean, correctly sized PDFs that the Sarathi and state RTO portals accept the first time. No software to install, no account to create — just your browser.

## What You'll Need

- Your documents — Aadhaar, PAN, birth certificate, or similar, as photos or scans
- A passport-size photograph and a sample of your signature
- A web browser on your phone or laptop
- No signup, no app, no payment

## Which Documents the Portal Actually Asks For

Before you convert anything, know what you are gathering. For a learner's license or driving license application through Sarathi (parivahan.gov.in) or your state RTO, you generally need three categories of proof.

| Category | Accepted documents (any one) | Purpose |
|----------|------------------------------|---------|
| Proof of address | Aadhaar, passport, voter ID, electricity bill, ration card | Confirms your residence |
| Proof of age / date of birth | Aadhaar, PAN, birth certificate, 10th marksheet, passport | Confirms you meet the minimum age |
| Identity + photo | Passport photo, signature image | Prints on the license |

Requirements vary slightly by state, so always check the exact list shown on your state's Sarathi page before you start. But the format problem is the same everywhere: your proofs are scattered as phone photos, PDFs, and screenshots, and the portal wants them uploaded in a specific way.

## The Format and Size Rules That Cause Rejections

Government portals are strict and often quiet about why an upload failed. Getting these right up front saves you the guesswork.

| Requirement | Typical rule on Sarathi / RTO portals |
|-------------|----------------------------------------|
| Document file type | PDF (some fields accept JPG separately) |
| Document file size | Usually under 500 KB per document, sometimes up to 1–2 MB |
| Photo file type | JPEG |
| Photo dimensions | Passport size, often around 3.5 × 4.5 cm equivalent |
| Signature file type | JPEG, on white background |
| Combined application cap | Often 5 MB total |

The two rules that catch people out most: documents usually need to be **PDF**, and each one usually needs to be **small** — often under 500 KB. A modern phone camera produces photos of 3 to 6 MB, so a raw snap of your Aadhaar card is roughly ten times too big. That is why simply converting to PDF is not enough; you often need to compress as well.

## Step-by-Step: Prepare Your Driving License Documents

### Step 1: Convert Your Document Photos to PDF

Most of your proofs — Aadhaar, birth certificate, marksheet — will be phone photos or scans. Turn each into a clean PDF.

1. Open the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf)
2. Upload the photo of one document
3. Set the page size to **A4, Portrait**, the standard for Indian government uploads
4. Convert and download, naming each file clearly:
   - `aadhaar-address-proof.pdf`
   - `birth-certificate-age-proof.pdf`
   - `pan-card.pdf`

Do each document separately unless the portal specifically asks for a combined file. Uploading one clean proof per field is what most Sarathi pages expect.

**Photo quality tips for ID documents:**
- Shoot in bright, even daylight near a window
- Lay the card or certificate flat on a dark surface for contrast
- Hold the phone directly overhead so the document is square, not tilted
- Make sure the name, number, and date of birth are all sharp and readable
- Capture the full document including any border, photo, or hologram

### Step 2: Compress Each PDF to Fit the Size Limit

A crisp A4 scan can still be 1 to 3 MB — over the portal's per-document limit. Shrink each one.

1. Open the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf)
2. Upload your document PDF
3. Start with **Medium compression**, then check the resulting size
4. If it is still above the limit, try **High compression**
5. Download and open the file to confirm every number and letter is still legible

The goal is a file comfortably under 500 KB where the Aadhaar number, name, and photo are all clearly readable. Compression that leaves the details blurry defeats the purpose — a clerk at the RTO still needs to verify it.

### Step 3: Prepare the Photo and Signature Correctly

The passport photo and signature are usually uploaded as **JPEG images**, not PDFs, so read the field label carefully before converting anything.

- **Photo:** a recent passport-size photograph on a light background, face clearly visible, no filters or heavy editing
- **Signature:** sign in black or dark blue ink on plain white paper, photograph it in good light, and crop it tightly

If a particular field on your state's portal asks for the photo or signature as a PDF instead, convert it with the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf). Otherwise leave them as JPEGs.

### Step 4: Combine Documents Only If the Portal Asks

Some state portals want a single combined PDF of all your proofs rather than separate uploads. If yours does:

1. Convert every document to PDF first
2. Open the [Merge PDF tool](/merge-pdf)
3. Arrange them in the order the portal lists (address proof, then age proof, then any extras)
4. Merge, then run the combined file through the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf) to stay under the 5 MB total cap

If the portal gives you separate upload boxes for each proof, skip merging and upload each file in its own box.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Uploading raw camera photos.** A 5 MB JPEG will almost always be rejected. Convert to PDF and compress first.
- **Ignoring the field label.** Some fields want PDF, others want JPEG. Read each one before uploading.
- **Over-compressing.** If the Aadhaar number or date of birth goes blurry, the RTO cannot verify it. Keep detail readable.
- **Wrong document for the field.** Address proof goes in the address field, age proof in the age field. Mixing them causes rejections.
- **Tilted or shadowed scans.** A crooked, dark photo of an ID looks unprofessional and may be refused. Shoot flat and bright.
- **Merging when the portal wants separate files.** Only combine documents if the portal explicitly asks for one file.
