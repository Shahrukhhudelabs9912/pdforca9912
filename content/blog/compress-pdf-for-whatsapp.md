## WhatsApp's Real-World Size Limits

WhatsApp's official document limit is 100 MB, but that number is not the real constraint. Here is what actually matters in practice:

| File size | What happens |

| Under 5 MB | Sends and opens instantly on any connection |

The sweet spot for reliable delivery across India — including smaller towns, older phones, and shared Wi-Fi — is under 10 MB. If the document is going to someone who might open it on a 2G connection or a budget Android phone, aim for under 5 MB.

## Why PDFs Get So Large

Before you compress, it helps to understand what is making your file big. Most oversized PDFs fall into one of these categories:

- **Scanned documents at high DPI** — A flatbed scanner set to 600 DPI produces beautiful images but enormous files. A 10-page scan at 600 DPI can easily be 20–40 MB.
- **Photos embedded in PDFs** — If you converted phone photos to PDF, each photo is stored at full camera resolution (often 12–48 megapixels). That is far more detail than a screen or printer needs.
- **Multiple merged files** — Combining several PDFs multiplies the size. A merged file of five scanned documents can hit 50 MB without anyone noticing.
- **Embedded fonts and metadata** — Less common, but some PDFs carry large embedded font sets or unnecessary metadata that adds bulk.

Compression works by reducing image resolution to screen-appropriate levels (typically 150 DPI for reading, 72 DPI for archiving) and stripping unnecessary data. Text and vector elements are not affected — they stay sharp at any zoom level.

## Tips for the Best Result

- Always send PDFs as **Document**, not as a photo or media file. The Document option preserves the PDF format, keeps it searchable, and lets the recipient zoom in on text.
- If you are compressing a file you will also need to print, use Medium compression — High compression can reduce print quality.
- For documents with fine print (legal agreements, medical reports, financial statements), zoom in on the compressed output before sending. A number that looks fine at 100% zoom may be blurry when printed.
- If your original file is already under 5 MB, compression may not be necessary. Check the file size before you start.
- Rename the compressed file clearly before sending — `property-agreement-compressed.pdf` is more useful to the recipient than `download(3).pdf`.
- For very large files (above 50 MB), consider splitting the document first using the [Split PDF tool](/split-pdf), compressing each part, and sending them separately.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is WhatsApp's actual PDF size limit?

WhatsApp allows documents up to 100 MB. However, files above 10 MB are slow to send and receive on mobile data connections, and files above 25 MB frequently time out on 2G or congested networks. ### Will compressing a PDF make the text blurry or unreadable?

Text and vector elements in a PDF are not affected by compression — they stay sharp at any zoom level. Compression reduces the resolution of embedded images (photos, scans, logos). At Medium compression, images are reduced to around 150 DPI, which is fully readable on screen and adequate for most printing. Always open the compressed file and zoom in on any figures, amounts, or fine print before sending.
