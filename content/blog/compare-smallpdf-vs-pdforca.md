You're looking for a free PDF tool and two names keep coming up: Smallpdf and PDFOrca. Both handle the common tasks — compress, merge, convert, split. But they're built on different models, and those differences matter depending on what you're doing and how sensitive your files are.

## Quick Overview

Smallpdf launched in 2013 and is one of the most recognized PDF tools on the web. It has a polished interface, a large feature set, and a freemium model — free with limits, paid for unlimited use.

PDFOrca is a newer, browser-based PDF tool suite. Everything runs locally in your browser: files are never uploaded to a server. It's free with no account required and no daily limits.

## Privacy: The Biggest Practical Difference

This is where the two tools diverge most significantly, and it's worth understanding clearly before you upload anything sensitive.

**Smallpdf** processes files on its servers. When you upload a PDF to Smallpdf, the file travels to their infrastructure, is processed there, and is stored temporarily (typically for one hour before deletion, though this varies by plan and feature). Smallpdf has a privacy policy and takes security seriously — but the fundamental model is server-side processing. Your file leaves your device.

**PDFOrca** processes files entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your file never leaves your device. There is no server receiving your upload, no temporary storage, nothing to breach on the backend. The processing happens locally, the same way a desktop app would work.

For most documents — a recipe PDF, a public report, a presentation — this distinction doesn't matter much. For sensitive documents — tax returns, medical records, contracts, ID documents, bank statements — it matters a great deal. A file that never leaves your device cannot be intercepted in transit, cannot be stored on a third-party server, and cannot be exposed in a data breach.

If you're handling sensitive documents, PDFOrca's browser-based model is a meaningful privacy advantage.

## Pricing

**Smallpdf** operates on a freemium model:

- Free tier: limited to 2 tasks per hour, files up to a certain size, no batch processing
- Smallpdf Pro: paid subscription (pricing varies by region, typically around $9–12/month billed annually) — removes limits, adds batch processing, desktop app, and priority processing
- Team plans available for multiple users

The free tier is usable for occasional tasks, but the two-tasks-per-hour limit becomes frustrating quickly if you're doing more than a couple of conversions in a session.

**PDFOrca** is free with no limits:

- No account required
- No daily or hourly task limits
- No file size caps beyond what your browser can handle
- No paid tier — all tools are free

If you're a regular user doing multiple PDF tasks, PDFOrca's unlimited free model is a practical advantage over Smallpdf's restricted free tier.

## Speed and Performance

Both tools are fast for standard tasks. The difference is in where the processing happens.

Smallpdf's server-side processing means upload and download time is part of the equation. On a fast connection, this is barely noticeable. On a slow or mobile connection, uploading a large PDF and waiting for the server to process it adds real time.

PDFOrca's browser-based processing means there's no upload step — the file is already on your device. For large files, this can be noticeably faster. The trade-off is that very complex operations may be slightly slower than server-side processing on a fast connection, since your device's CPU is doing the work instead of a server.

For most users on a reasonable connection, both tools feel fast. PDFOrca has an edge on slow connections and for large files.

## Ease of Use

Both tools have clean, straightforward interfaces. You upload a file, choose an action, download the result. Neither requires technical knowledge.

Smallpdf has a more polished visual design and a longer track record of UX refinement. It also has a unified dashboard if you create an account, which makes it easier to manage multiple files and tasks.

PDFOrca is simpler — each tool is a standalone page, there's no account, and there's no dashboard. This is either a feature or a limitation depending on your workflow. If you want to do one thing quickly without logging in, it's faster. If you want to manage a batch of files across multiple operations, Smallpdf's account-based workflow is more organized.

## Real-World Scenarios

### Occasional Personal Use

You need to compress a PDF to email it, or merge two documents for a job application. Either tool works fine. PDFOrca is faster to reach (no account needed) and has no task limits.

### Regular Professional Use

You're processing PDFs daily — converting reports, compressing files, splitting documents. Smallpdf's free tier will hit its hourly limit quickly. PDFOrca handles unlimited tasks for free. If you need batch processing or a desktop app, Smallpdf Pro is worth considering.

### Sensitive Documents

You're compressing a tax return, converting a medical record, or protecting a contract. PDFOrca's browser-based processing means the file never leaves your device. For this use case, PDFOrca is the better choice on privacy grounds alone.

### Team Workflow

You need multiple people to process PDFs with shared access and a unified account. Smallpdf has team plans designed for this. PDFOrca doesn't have account or team features — each user works independently.

### Slow or Mobile Connection

You're on a mobile data connection or a slow network. PDFOrca's local processing avoids the upload/download cycle, which makes it faster in this scenario.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is PDFOrca really free with no limits?

Yes. PDFOrca is free, requires no account, and has no daily or hourly task limits. All tools are available without payment or signup.

### Does Smallpdf store my files?

Smallpdf processes files on its servers and stores them temporarily — typically for one hour before deletion. The exact retention period depends on the feature and plan. Check Smallpdf's current privacy policy for the latest details.

### Can PDFOrca handle large files?

PDFOrca processes files in your browser, so the practical limit depends on your device's memory and browser. Most standard PDF tasks work fine. Very large files (hundreds of MB) may be slower or hit browser memory limits on older devices.

### Which tool is better for OCR?

Both offer OCR for scanned PDFs. PDFOrca's [OCR PDF tool](/ocr-pdf) is free with no limits. Smallpdf's OCR is available on paid plans. For free OCR without limits, PDFOrca has the advantage.

### Does PDFOrca have a mobile app?

No. PDFOrca is browser-based and works in any mobile browser, but there's no dedicated app. Smallpdf has iOS and Android apps if a native mobile experience matters to you.
