Every job portal, every government exam application, every university admission form eventually asks the same thing: upload all your academic documents as a single PDF.

You have six separate files — 10th marksheet, 12th marksheet, four semester results. The portal has one upload slot. And it closes in an hour.

This guide gets you from scattered marksheets to one clean, properly ordered PDF in under 3 minutes.

## What You'll Need

- Your marksheets as images (phone photos) or individual PDFs
- A browser — phone or laptop
- No signup, no app, no payment

## The Typical Document Set

Most Indian job and exam applications ask for a standard set of academic documents:

| Document | Why it's needed |
|----------|----------------|
| 10th / SSC marksheet | Proof of date of birth, basic qualification |
| 12th / HSC marksheet | Intermediate qualification |
| Degree certificate | Final qualification proof |
| Semester marksheets (all) | Detailed academic performance |
| Consolidated marksheet | Overall grade/percentage summary |
| Provisional certificate | If final degree not yet issued |

Some applications want all of these in one PDF. Others want specific combinations. Read the portal instructions carefully before merging — uploading extra documents wastes the reviewer's time and can slow processing.

## Step-by-Step: Marksheets to One PDF

### If Your Marksheets Are Photos (JPG/PNG)

Most people have phone photos of their marksheets. Convert them to PDF first:

1. Open the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf)
2. Upload each marksheet image one at a time
3. Set page size to **A4, Portrait** (standard for Indian documents)
4. Convert and save each as a separate PDF:
   - `10th-marksheet.pdf`
   - `12th-marksheet.pdf`
   - `degree-semester-1.pdf`
   - ...and so on

**Photo quality tips:**
- Photograph in good lighting — natural daylight is best
- Place the marksheet on a dark, flat surface for contrast
- Hold your phone directly above, not at an angle
- Make sure the entire document is visible, including edges and institution seal
- Zoom in slightly to fill the frame

### If Your Marksheets Are Already PDFs

Skip straight to the merge step. DigiLocker, university portals, and online result systems often provide PDFs directly — use those when available.

### Merge All Into One PDF

1. Open the [Merge PDF tool](/merge-pdf)
2. Upload all your marksheet PDFs
3. **Arrange in chronological order** — this matters:
   - 10th marksheet (oldest first)
   - 12th marksheet
   - Semester 1, 2, 3... (in order)
   - Consolidated marksheet
   - Degree / Provisional certificate (last)
4. Click **Merge** and download

You now have one PDF with every marksheet in order — exactly what portals expect.

## Getting the Order Right

Chronological order isn't just a suggestion — it's what reviewers expect, and some automated systems check for it. A scrambled order can delay verification.

**Recommended order:**

```
Page 1:  10th / SSC marksheet
Page 2:  12th / HSC marksheet
Page 3:  Semester 1 marksheet
Page 4:  Semester 2 marksheet
...
Page N:  Final semester marksheet
Page N+1: Consolidated marksheet
Page N+2: Degree / Provisional certificate
```

If the portal specifies a different order, follow their instructions. When in doubt, chronological is always safe.

## Meeting Upload Size Limits

Combined marksheets can easily cross 10–20 MB, especially if the originals are high-resolution scans. Most portals have strict limits:

| Portal / Application | Typical size limit |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Government job portals (SSC, UPSC, Railways) | 200 KB – 2 MB |
| State PSC applications | 500 KB – 1 MB |
| Private job portals (Naukri, LinkedIn) | 2–5 MB |
| University admissions | 2–5 MB |
| Scholarship applications | 500 KB – 2 MB |
| Bank recruitment (IBPS, SBI PO) | 200 KB – 1 MB |

If your merged PDF exceeds the limit:

1. Open the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf)
2. Upload the merged file
3. Start with **Medium compression** — usually enough for 2–5 MB limits
4. For strict limits (200 KB – 1 MB), use **High compression**
5. Download and check that text is still readable

A 15 MB merged file typically compresses to 2–4 MB at Medium, or under 1 MB at High.

## Real-World Scenarios

### SSC / UPSC Exam Applications

Government competitive exams ask for all academic documents during the form-filling stage. The upload window is typically tight — forms close on a deadline and the server is under heavy load.

**Prepare in advance:**
1. Convert all marksheets to PDF
2. Merge in order
3. Compress to under the stated limit
4. Save the ready-to-upload file as `academic-documents-SSC.pdf`

Having this file ready before the application window opens saves you from scrambling on the last day.

### Campus Placement Document Verification

During campus placements, companies ask students to submit all academic records as a single PDF for verification. The placement cell usually specifies the order and format.

1. Collect all semester marksheets + 10th + 12th
2. Merge in the specified order
3. Compress if needed
4. Submit before the deadline

### MBA / Higher Education Admissions

CAT, XAT, GMAT, and other entrance exam applications require academic records. University admission portals (IIMs, NITs, central universities) want everything in one file:

1. 10th and 12th marksheets
2. Undergraduate degree and marksheets
3. Any postgraduate marksheets (if applicable)
4. Merge and compress to the portal's limit

### Government Job Applications (State PSC, Railways, Banking)

State Public Service Commissions, Railway Recruitment Boards, and banking recruitment (IBPS, SBI) all have online applications with document upload requirements.

**Common pitfalls:**
- Uploading files larger than the limit (form silently fails or rejects)
- Wrong document order (delays verification)
- Blurry marksheet images (application rejected during document verification)
- Missing a marksheet (incomplete application)

Prepare your merged PDF well before the deadline. Don't wait until the last day — server crashes on deadline day are routine.

## Using DigiLocker for Marksheets

DigiLocker (digilocker.gov.in) is the Indian government's official digital document wallet. Many universities and boards now issue marksheets directly to DigiLocker:

- **CBSE** — 10th and 12th marksheets
- **Many state boards** — HSC and SSC marksheets
- **Some universities** — Degree and semester marksheets

**To check:** Log in to DigiLocker → Issued Documents → search for your board or university.

DigiLocker documents are already proper PDFs, often with digital signatures. Download them and merge directly — no need to photograph and convert.

**Advantage:** DigiLocker-issued documents are accepted everywhere and carry government-verified digital signatures, which some portals specifically prefer over scanned copies.

## Common Mistakes That Get Applications Rejected

**Blurry or dark marksheet photos.** If the registration number, marks, and institution name aren't clearly readable, the document verification team will reject your application. Retake the photo in better lighting.

**Missing pages.** If your marksheet has a front and back, include both. A front-only upload missing the subject-wise breakdown on the back is incomplete.

**Wrong file format.** The portal says PDF — upload PDF. Uploading JPG images when PDF is required is an instant rejection. Always convert images to PDF using [JPG to PDF](/jpg-to-pdf) first.

**Exceeding the size limit.** An oversized file either fails silently (you think it uploaded but it didn't) or throws an error on the last step. Always compress to within the stated limit.

**Including unrelated documents.** If the portal asks for "academic marksheets," don't include your Aadhaar, PAN card, or experience certificates in the same PDF. Only merge what's asked for — extra documents confuse the reviewer.

**Landscape marksheets uploaded sideways.** Some university marksheets are printed in landscape format. If they appear sideways in your PDF, rotate them using the [Rotate PDF tool](/rotate-pdf) before merging.

## After Merging: Quality Checklist

Before uploading your merged PDF, open it and check:

- [ ] All marksheets are included (no missing semesters)
- [ ] Pages are in chronological order
- [ ] Every page is the right way up (not sideways or upside down)
- [ ] Text is readable when zoomed to 100%
- [ ] Registration numbers and marks are clearly visible
- [ ] Institution seals and signatures are visible
- [ ] File size is within the portal's upload limit
- [ ] File format is PDF (not JPG or PNG)

This checklist takes 60 seconds and prevents rejections that cost weeks.

## FAQ

**How many marksheets can I merge at once?**
No practical limit. Whether it's 4 marksheets or 20, the merge tool handles them all. Larger sets take a few extra seconds to process.

**Should I include marksheets for failed attempts?**
Only if the portal specifically asks for all attempts. Most applications want only the passing marksheet for each level.

**Can I merge marksheets from different boards/universities?**
Absolutely. 10th from CBSE, 12th from a state board, degree from a university — they all merge into one PDF regardless of source.

**My consolidated marksheet has multiple pages — how do I handle it?**
If it's already a multi-page PDF, upload it as-is during the merge step. If you have separate photos of each page, convert each to PDF individually, then merge everything together.

**The portal asks for "self-attested" marksheets — what does that mean?**
Write "Self-Attested" and your signature on each physical marksheet, then photograph the signed version. Convert to PDF and merge as usual.

**Can I rearrange pages after merging?**
Yes. Use the [Organize PDF tool](/organize-pdf) to drag pages into the correct order if you merged them wrong.
