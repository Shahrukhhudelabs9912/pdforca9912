You're filling out an online application — a college admission form, a job portal, a government scheme, a bank account opening — and the last step asks you to upload a photo ID proof in PDF format, under 500 KB or 1 MB. Your Aadhaar card is a physical card in your wallet. Your PAN card is a laminated piece of plastic. You have a photo on your phone, but the form won't accept JPG.

This is one of the most common friction points in India's digital application ecosystem. Almost every government portal, university admission system, and private sector onboarding flow asks for ID proof as a PDF. Yet most people's ID documents exist as physical cards, downloaded images, or DigiLocker files — not as ready-to-upload PDFs.

This guide shows you exactly how to convert any photo ID — Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, passport, driving licence — into a clean, correctly sized PDF that any online form will accept.

## What You'll Need

- A photo or scan of your ID document (front and back if required)
- A web browser on your phone or laptop
- No signup, no app, no payment

## Which ID Documents Are Commonly Required

Different applications ask for different documents. Here's what's typically accepted as photo ID proof in India:

| Document | Common use case | Format usually required |
|----------|----------------|------------------------|
| Aadhaar card | Most government portals, bank KYC, college admissions | PDF, under 1 MB |
| PAN card | Income tax, financial applications, job portals | PDF or JPG |
| Voter ID (EPIC) | Electoral rolls, some government schemes | PDF |
| Passport | Visa applications, international travel, premium KYC | PDF |
| Driving licence | Vehicle-related applications, address proof | PDF |
| College/school ID | Student portals, scholarship applications | PDF or JPG |

Most portals accept Aadhaar as the primary ID. If you have your Aadhaar downloaded from UIDAI as a PDF already, you may just need to compress it. If you only have a photo or scan, you need to convert it first.

## Step-by-Step: Convert Your Photo ID to PDF

### Step 1: Get a Clear Photo or Scan of Your ID

The quality of your source image determines the quality of your PDF. A blurry or dark photo will produce an unreadable PDF that may be rejected.

For best results:
- Place the ID card on a flat, well-lit surface
- Use natural light or a bright lamp — avoid flash glare
- Hold your phone directly above the card, not at an angle
- Capture the full card with a small border around it
- For documents that require both sides (like Aadhaar or driving licence), photograph each side separately

If your phone has a document scanner (Google PhotoScan, iPhone's built-in scanner in Notes, or Microsoft Lens), use it — it automatically corrects perspective and improves contrast.

### Step 2: Convert the Photo to PDF

Open the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf) in your browser.

1. Upload your ID photo (JPG or PNG)
2. If the document requires both front and back, upload both images together — they'll become two pages in the PDF
3. Click Convert and download the PDF

The tool works entirely in your browser. Your ID image is not stored on any server.

### Step 3: Check the File Size

Most portals have a file size limit — commonly 500 KB, 1 MB, or 2 MB. After converting, check the PDF size.

If it's too large, run it through the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf). A photo of an Aadhaar card converted to PDF is typically 200–800 KB depending on the original image resolution. Compression usually brings it well within any portal's limit without making the text unreadable.

### Step 4: Verify the PDF Before Uploading

Open the PDF and check:
- Is the full card visible, including all four corners?
- Is the text readable — name, ID number, date of birth?
- Is the photo on the ID clear?
- If two-sided, are both pages present?

A rejected upload wastes time. Thirty seconds of checking saves a resubmission.

## Specific Documents: How to Handle Each One

### Aadhaar Card

The easiest option is to download your e-Aadhaar directly from the UIDAI website (uidai.gov.in). It comes as a password-protected PDF — the password is your PIN code. Most portals accept this directly.

If the portal does not accept password-protected PDFs, use the [Unlock PDF tool](/unlock-pdf) to remove the password before uploading.

If you only have a physical Aadhaar card, photograph both sides clearly and convert them to PDF using the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf). Upload both images together so front and back appear on separate pages of the same PDF.

### PAN Card

PAN cards are typically required as a single-page document showing both sides. Photograph front and back, then upload both images to the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf) to get a two-page PDF. Some portals want both sides on a single page — in that case, place the card on a scanner or use a collage app to put both sides side by side before converting.

### Voter ID (EPIC Card)

Voter ID cards are issued as physical cards. Photograph both sides in good light. Convert using the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf). The card number, name, and photo must all be clearly visible.

### Passport

If you have a scanned copy of your passport's data page (the page with your photo), convert it to PDF. If you are using a photo, make sure the entire page is captured — including the machine-readable zone at the bottom. Passport copies are often required for visa applications and premium KYC processes.

### Driving Licence

Driving licences issued after 2019 in India are smart card format. Photograph both sides. For older booklet-format licences, photograph the relevant pages. Convert to PDF with the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf).

## Real-World Scenarios

### Applying for a Scholarship Online

Most scholarship portals — NSP (National Scholarship Portal), state government portals, private foundations — require Aadhaar as ID proof in PDF format under 500 KB.

1. Download e-Aadhaar from UIDAI as PDF
2. If it is password-protected, remove the password with the [Unlock PDF tool](/unlock-pdf)
3. If the file is over 500 KB, compress it with the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf)
4. Upload to the portal

### Opening a Bank Account or Completing KYC

Banks and financial apps require ID proof during account opening or KYC updates. They typically accept Aadhaar or PAN as PDF.

1. For Aadhaar: download e-Aadhaar from UIDAI, remove password if needed
2. For PAN: photograph both sides, convert to PDF with the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf)
3. Check the portal's size limit — most bank portals accept up to 2 MB
4. Upload each document to the correct field

### College or University Admission Forms

Admission portals often ask for multiple ID documents — Aadhaar, school leaving certificate, and a passport-size photo — each as a separate PDF.

1. Convert your Aadhaar photo to PDF using the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf)
2. If you have a scanned school certificate, convert it the same way
3. For the passport-size photo, the portal usually accepts JPG directly — check before converting
4. Keep each document as a separate file; do not merge them unless the portal asks for a combined document

### Government Job Application (SSC, UPSC, State PSC)

Government recruitment portals have strict format requirements. SSC and UPSC portals typically require ID proof as PDF under 1 MB, with specific dimensions sometimes specified.

1. Download e-Aadhaar or photograph your Voter ID
2. Convert to PDF using the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf)
3. Compress if needed — government portals often have tight size limits
4. Double-check the portal's specific requirements before uploading; some ask for JPG, not PDF

## Tips for the Best Result

- **Use e-Aadhaar from UIDAI** rather than photographing your physical card — it is already a clean PDF and is officially accepted everywhere
- **Photograph in landscape orientation** for cards — it captures the full card without cropping
- **Use a plain white or dark background** behind the card — it makes the edges clear and improves contrast
- **Avoid flash** — it creates glare on laminated cards and makes text hard to read
- **Check the portal's accepted formats** before converting — some portals accept JPG directly and do not need a PDF
- **Keep the original photos** after converting — if you need to resubmit or apply elsewhere, you will not need to photograph the card again
- **Name files clearly** — `aadhaar-front-back.pdf`, `pan-card.pdf` — so you can find them quickly across multiple applications

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Blurry or dark photos** — the ID number and name must be clearly readable; retake if in doubt
- **Cutting off card edges** — capture the full card with a small border; cropped corners can cause rejection
- **Uploading the wrong side** — some portals specifically ask for the front only; others want both sides
- **Forgetting to remove the Aadhaar password** — e-Aadhaar PDFs are password-protected by default; portals often cannot open them
- **Exceeding the file size limit** — compress before uploading if the PDF is over the portal's limit
- **Using an expired document** — check that your ID is valid before uploading, especially for driving licences

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use a photo of my Aadhaar card instead of downloading the e-Aadhaar PDF?

Yes, a clear photo of your Aadhaar card converted to PDF is accepted by most portals. However, the e-Aadhaar PDF downloaded from UIDAI is the preferred format — it is cleaner, officially issued, and already in PDF format. Use the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf) to convert a photo if you do not have access to the UIDAI portal.

### My e-Aadhaar PDF is password-protected. How do I remove the password?

The e-Aadhaar password is your 8-digit PIN code (the PIN you set when downloading). If the portal cannot open it, use the [Unlock PDF tool](/unlock-pdf) to remove the password. Enter your PIN when prompted, and the tool will produce an unlocked PDF you can upload freely.

### The portal says my PDF is too large. What should I do?

Run your PDF through the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf). A photo of an ID card converted to PDF is typically 300 KB to 1 MB. Compression usually brings it well under any portal's limit — often to under 200 KB — without making the text unreadable.

### Do I need to upload both sides of my Aadhaar or PAN card?

It depends on the portal. Most portals that ask for Aadhaar accept either the front only or both sides. PAN card uploads usually require both sides. Read the portal's instructions carefully — if it says "both sides," upload front and back as two pages in the same PDF using the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf).

### Can I use my DigiLocker documents instead of converting photos?

Yes, if the portal accepts DigiLocker-issued documents. DigiLocker provides officially issued PDFs for Aadhaar, driving licence, and other documents. Download the PDF from DigiLocker and upload it directly. If the portal does not accept DigiLocker links and requires a file upload, download the PDF from DigiLocker and upload that file.

### How do I upload a Voter ID (EPIC card) to a government portal?

Voter ID cards are physical cards with no official digital download option equivalent to e-Aadhaar. Photograph both the front and back of the card in good natural light, making sure the card number, your name, and your photo are all clearly visible. Upload both images together to the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf) so they become a single two-page PDF. If the portal has a size limit, compress the PDF with the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf) before uploading — a two-page Voter ID PDF typically compresses to well under 500 KB without any loss of readability.

### How do I prepare a passport copy for a visa application or premium KYC?

For most applications, only the data page of your passport is required — the page with your photograph, name, date of birth, and passport number. If you have a scanned copy, convert it to PDF with the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf). If you are photographing it, lay the passport flat and open to the data page, shoot directly overhead in good light, and make sure the machine-readable zone at the bottom of the page is fully captured and legible. Some visa applications also ask for the address page or the last page showing any existing visas — check the specific requirements before converting. Compress the final PDF if the portal has a size limit.

### Can I reuse the same ID proof PDF across multiple applications?

Yes, and this is one of the main advantages of converting your ID to PDF once and saving it. A PDF of your Aadhaar, PAN, or Voter ID can be uploaded to any portal that accepts that document type. Keep clearly named files — `aadhaar-front-back.pdf`, `pan-card.pdf`, `voter-id.pdf` — in a dedicated folder on your phone and laptop. The only time you need to reconvert is if the document expires (driving licence, passport) or if you get a new card. For Aadhaar, the e-Aadhaar PDF from UIDAI is always current, so re-download it if your details change.

### What if the portal only accepts JPG and not PDF?

Some portals — particularly older government recruitment portals — specify JPG rather than PDF for photo ID uploads. In that case, do not convert to PDF; upload the original JPG directly. If the JPG is too large, reduce its resolution or use an image compression tool before uploading. Check the portal's instructions carefully before converting anything — the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf) is for portals that require PDF, not for those that explicitly want JPG. When a portal accepts both, PDF is generally the safer choice because it preserves the full image without further compression.

### My portal rejected my PDF even though the size is within the limit. What else could be wrong?

Size is only one of several reasons a portal rejects an upload. Other common causes: the PDF is password-protected (use the [Unlock PDF tool](/unlock-pdf) to remove the password), the file is corrupted or incomplete (re-convert from the original image), the portal expects a specific PDF version that older tools do not produce (try re-converting with the [JPG to PDF tool](/jpg-to-pdf)), or the portal's file-type check is strict and the file extension does not match the actual format. If none of these apply, try a different browser — some older government portals have upload issues with certain browsers.
