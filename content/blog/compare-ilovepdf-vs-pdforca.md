## Privacy: The Real Difference

This is where the two tools diverge most, and it is the reason PDFOrca exists.

When you use a traditional online PDF service, your file is uploaded to a server, processed there, and then made available for download. The company then deletes it, usually after a set number of hours, and reputable services like iLovePDF are transparent about their retention and use encrypted connections. That is a reasonable model, and for most everyday documents it is perfectly fine.

But "uploaded to a server and deleted later" is a fundamentally different trust model from "never left your computer." For sensitive documents — a salary slip, an Aadhaar card, a signed contract, medical records, bank statements — a lot of people are rightly uncomfortable sending the file to any third party, no matter how well-run.

PDFOrca leans into browser-based processing so that for many tasks, your file is handled locally by your own browser and never travels to a server at all. There is nothing to delete later because nothing was uploaded. That is the core of the privacy pitch, and it is a real, structural difference rather than a policy promise.

To be balanced: iLovePDF's server model enables features that are difficult to do purely in the browser, and their security practices are mature. If your document is not sensitive, the difference may not matter to you. But if it is, the distinction is worth understanding.

| Privacy question | PDFOrca | iLovePDF |
|------------------|---------|----------|
| File uploaded to a server? | Often no, processed in browser | Yes, for most tasks |
| Retention to worry about? | Nothing stored when local | Files deleted after a set period |
| Account/tracking to opt out of | Minimal | Standard analytics + optional account |
| Best for sensitive documents | Strong fit | Depends on your comfort |

## Speed: What Actually Feels Faster

Speed is not one number. It depends on your file, your device, and your internet connection.

For a typical document on a decent computer, **browser-based processing can feel instant** because there is no upload-and-download round trip. You pick the file, the work happens locally, and you save the result. On a slow connection, avoiding a large upload is a genuine time saver.

Server-based processing, on the other hand, shines with **very large files or heavy batches** on a powerful connection. A data center has far more horsepower than a phone, so a giant compression job or a hundred-file batch can finish faster on the server side once the upload is done.

For the typical user — a single document, a normal laptop, a home or office connection — the speed difference is not dramatic either way. Both tools feel fast for everyday tasks.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Uploading sensitive documents to any server-based tool without checking the privacy policy.** Know where your file goes before you upload it.
- **Assuming "free" means the same thing on every tool.** iLovePDF's free tier has limits; PDFOrca's does not currently.
- **Using a browser-based tool for a 500 MB file.** Very large files are better handled server-side. Know your file size before you start.
- **Ignoring the output quality.** Always open the result and check it before sending. Both tools produce good output, but compression settings affect readability.
