You've filled out the UPSC online application form, scanned your documents, and you're ready to upload — then the portal throws an error: "File size exceeds the limit." The document is 4MB. The portal wants 300KB.

This is one of the most common frustrations for government exam applicants in India. UPSC, NTA, SSC, IBPS, and state PSC portals all have strict file size limits, and the limits vary by document type. A scanned certificate that looks fine on your screen can be several times larger than what the portal accepts.

The fix is straightforward: compress the PDF before uploading. This guide walks through exactly how to do it, what limits to expect on major portals, and how to compress without making your documents unreadable.

## Typical Size Limits on Government Portals

Every government portal sets its own limits, and they change from year to year and exam to exam. Always check the official notification for your specific exam. That said, here are the typical ranges you'll encounter:

| Portal / Exam | Document Type | Typical Size Limit |
|---|---|---|
| UPSC (Civil Services, etc.) | Photograph | 20 KB – 300 KB |
| UPSC | Signature | 20 KB – 300 KB |
| UPSC | Certificates / documents | Up to 1–2 MB |
| NTA (JEE, NEET, UGC-NET) | Photograph | 10 KB – 200 KB |
| NTA | Signature | 4 KB – 30 KB |
| NTA | Category / PwD certificate | 50 KB – 300 KB |
| SSC (CGL, CHSL, etc.) | Photograph | 20 KB – 50 KB |
| SSC | Signature | 10 KB – 20 KB |
| IBPS (bank exams) | Photograph | 20 KB – 50 KB |
| IBPS | Signature / handwriting | 10 KB – 50 KB |
| State PSC portals | Documents | Often under 1 MB |

The pattern is clear: photos and signatures need to be very small (often under 50KB), while document PDFs get a bit more room (up to 1-2MB). The exact number in your exam notification is the one that counts, so read it before you compress.

A second thing to watch: many portals also cap the dimensions and format, not just the size. A photo might need to be JPEG at specific pixel dimensions, and a document might need to be PDF specifically. Check both.

## Why Your PDF Is Too Big

Most government exam applicants scan their documents at home or at a cyber cafe, and the default scanner settings are designed for print quality — not web uploads. Here's what inflates the file size:

Scanning at 300 DPI or higher is the most common cause. 300 DPI is appropriate for printing, but government portals display documents on screen at 72–96 DPI. Scanning at 300 DPI creates a file 9–16 times larger than necessary for screen viewing, with no visible benefit on the portal.

Scanning in color adds significant size for documents that are black and white. A color scan of a certificate or mark sheet is typically 3–5 times larger than a grayscale scan of the same document.

Using PNG instead of JPEG for photographs also inflates size. PNG is lossless and much larger; JPEG compresses photographs efficiently with minimal visible quality loss.

Finally, multi-page PDFs accumulate size quickly. A 10-page document scanned at 300 DPI in color can easily reach 15–20MB — far beyond any portal's limit.

### NTA (JEE/NEET) Application

NTA portals are among the strictest — photographs often need to be under 200KB and signatures under 30KB.

1. For the photograph: scan or photograph at 200 DPI, compress to under 200KB
2. For the signature: scan on white paper at 150 DPI in grayscale, compress aggressively
3. Verify both files meet the pixel dimension requirements in the NTA notification (usually 3.5cm × 4.5cm for photos)

### SSC CGL/CHSL Application

SSC portals typically want photographs under 50KB and signatures under 20KB — very tight limits.

1. Photograph: use a recent passport-size photo, scan at 100 DPI in color, compress
2. Signature: sign on white paper with a black pen, scan at 100 DPI in grayscale
3. Both should compress well under the limits with medium compression

### Uploading a Certificate or Mark Sheet

For document PDFs (caste certificate, degree certificate, mark sheet), portals usually allow 500KB to 2MB.

1. Scan the document at 150 DPI in grayscale
2. Compress with the [Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf)
3. A single A4 page scanned at 150 DPI in grayscale typically compresses to under 200KB easily

## Tips for Passing Portal Checks

- Always read the official exam notification for the exact size limit — don't guess
- Compress to slightly under the limit, not exactly at it — portals sometimes measure differently
- Check readability after compressing — text and signatures must be clearly legible
- Scan in grayscale for black-and-white documents; color adds file size without benefit
- Scan at 150 DPI for documents, not 300 DPI — 300 DPI is for print, not screen display
- Keep the original uncompressed scan — you may need it later for verification
- If the portal rejects your file even after compression, check whether it requires a specific format (PDF vs. JPEG) or specific pixel dimensions
- Use a phone scanner app set to document mode — these produce smaller, cleaner files than flatbed scans at high DPI
